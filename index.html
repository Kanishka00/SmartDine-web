Smart Dine

Smart Dine is an AI-powered, all-in-one restaurant management tool designed to streamline operations, enhance customer experiences, and optimize workflow efficiency. Smart Dine provides an intelligent solution to modernize and simplify restaurant management.

Domain

Smart Dine provides an in-depth look at the specific industry focus within which it operates, primarily targeting the food and beverage sector. This section outlines the primary audience, including restaurant owners, managers, and hospitality professionals, while addressing the market needs that Smart Dine aims to fulfill. By discussing common challenges faced in the industry such as enhancing customer experience and improving operational efficiency

Objectives

Enhancing Customer Satisfaction

SmartDine aims to improve the dining experience by focusing on customer satisfaction. It does this through real-time analysis of key metrics such as food waste, customer engagement, and wait time reduction. By using CCTV footage and machine learning, the system provides actionable insights that help restaurants offer better service, leading to higher customer retention and loyalty.

Optimizing Business Operations

The system streamlines restaurant operations by enhancing resource management, table turnover, and staff-customer interactions. Predictive models help optimize labor allocation, menu item predictions, and reservation systems to ensure smoother operations during peak times and more efficient use of resources.

LITERATURE REVIEW

Research consistently shows that high service quality and customer satisfaction enhance firm profitability, boost staff productivity, and reduce costs. In the context of restaurants, customer satisfaction is often measured through various dimensions, including food quality, service quality, ambiance, price fairness, and convenience.

Research gap

1. Customer Turnaround Time Analysis While previous studies have highlighted the importance of reducing customer wait times to improve satisfaction, most approaches rely on traditional time tracking or manual observations. There is limited research on using computer vision technology for real-time monitoring of customer turnaround times. Integrating predictive analytics with video analysis to optimize service intervals remains an underexplored area.

2. Staff-Customer Interaction Analysis Although studies have shown the impact of positive staff-customer interactions on customer satisfaction, existing methods primarily use customer feedback or manual observation. There is a lack of research utilizing computer vision to objectively monitor and quantify these interactions in real time. Automating the analysis of these interactions using AI could provide more consistent and scalable insights.

3. Food Leftover Analysis Current research on food waste reduction often focuses on survey-based data to understand customer satisfaction with meal portions. Limited work has been done to employ computer vision for real-time analysis of leftover food on plates. Using image processing to measure food waste and relate it directly to customer satisfaction and portion optimization is a relatively unexplored approach.

4. Customer Engagement Analysis Traditional methods of measuring customer engagement rely on qualitative data such as surveys, or quantitative metrics like the time spent in the restaurant. While there is some research on using behavioral cues (e.g., eye tracking) to assess engagement, integrating computer vision for a comprehensive analysis of engagement behaviors at the table (e.g., body language, movement) is still an emerging area.

5. Table Turnover Prediction Studies have explored strategies to optimize table turnover for operational efficiency, but they typically involve static rule-based approaches or use POS data alone. There is limited research on using real-time video analysis combined with predictive analytics to forecast table turnover. Integrating multiple data sources, including visual cues from CCTV, for more accurate turnover predictions is not well-documented.

6. Prediction for Dynamic Resource Allocation Current approaches to resource allocation in restaurants primarily use sales and historical data for forecasting, which may not capture the dynamic nature of restaurant environments. While previous studies have applied labor forecasting techniques from other industries, such as construction, there is a lack of focus on predicting labor demand specifically for various employment types (e.g., part-time vs. full-time staff) in the restaurant context. Additionally, these methods do not typically incorporate external factors, such as special events, holidays or real-time changes in customer traffic. There is also limited research on using machine learning models that integrate multiple data to adjust resource allocation dynamically in response to changing operational needs.

Research Problem How can traditional restaurant management tackle operational inefficiencies and enhance customer satisfaction by reducing manual operations, optimizing resource allocation, and improving adaptability to change?

METHODOLOGY
